# Essay Feedback Rubric

## Allowed Feedback

Review for:
- Authenticity
- Specificity
- Motivation clarity
- Program fit
- Evidence strength
- Career coherence
- Structure and flow
- Repetition with CV
- Risk of exaggeration
- Plagiarism or over-borrowing risk

## Feedback Format

Use:
- Overall diagnosis
- Top 3 revision priorities
- Section-level comments
- Evidence gaps
- Program-fit suggestions
- Questions for the student to answer

When the user asks for essay story direction, provide logical frameworks rather than full essay drafts unless the user explicitly asks for a draft. For multi-track applicants, give several direction-specific story frameworks across the applicant's actual domains, including humanities, business, engineering, science, data, education, law, and public policy when relevant.

## Cross-Discipline Story Frameworks

Use these as adaptable logic structures. Replace examples with the user's actual experiences.

### Applied Linguistics / Professional English

Core thesis: Language is not only expression; it is a professional tool for understanding, coordinating, and influencing people across contexts.

Logic chain:

```text
English major
→ language, text, and cross-cultural foundation
→ journalism or media experience
→ public information organization and audience understanding
→ agency/client-facing experience
→ professional communication, brand language, stakeholder coordination
→ marketing or consumer-facing experience
→ language as a bridge between consumer feedback and market communication
→ academic interest
→ applied linguistics / professional English / workplace communication
→ career goal
→ professional communication, brand communication, international communication, language services
```

Key questions:
- Why does the applicant need graduate training beyond an English major?
- Why applied language or professional English instead of pure linguistics?
- Which real language-use contexts has the applicant observed?
- What communication problem does the applicant want to study or solve?

### Communication / IMC / Media

Core thesis: The applicant wants to understand how information moves from being expressed to being understood, accepted, and acted on by an audience.

Logic chain:

```text
English major
→ sensitivity to text, narrative, culture, and audience
→ journalism/media experience
→ fact selection, interviewing, storytelling, public communication
→ advertising or agency experience
→ brand communication, client briefs, campaign execution
→ marketing or brand-side experience
→ communication effectiveness, consumer feedback, market context
→ academic interest
→ IMC / media management / strategic communication
→ career goal
→ brand communication, content strategy, international communication, integrated marketing
```

Key questions:
- What changed when the applicant moved from public/news communication to brand communication?
- How does the applicant understand "audience"?
- Why is IMC or strategic communication the right next step?
- Does the career goal point to content, brand, media, or strategy?

### Marketing / Brand Management

Core thesis: The applicant moves from language and communication toward consumer understanding, brand strategy, and market decision-making.

Logic chain:

```text
English major
→ cross-cultural expression, audience awareness, text analysis
→ journalism/media experience
→ public attention and information judgment
→ advertising/agency experience
→ brand campaigns, client needs, creative execution
→ marketing/brand-side experience
→ consumer scenarios, brand activity, market execution
→ market research or business competition
→ research methods, consumer analysis, evidence-based thinking
→ academic interest
→ marketing / brand management / consumer behaviour
→ career goal
→ brand management, market strategy, consumer insight, international marketing
```

Key questions:
- Why is the shift from English to marketing reasonable?
- What evidence shows consumer or brand understanding?
- What market-research evidence supports the application?
- Which business methods does the applicant need to learn next?

### Marketing Analytics / Consumer Insight

Core thesis: The applicant wants to turn communication instinct into testable, data-informed consumer insight.

Logic chain:

```text
English major and media experience
→ audience awareness and information expression
→ advertising/agency experience
→ how brands reach consumers
→ marketing/brand-side experience
→ real market context and consumer feedback
→ market research competition or project
→ consumer judgment requires research and data
→ current gap
→ need stronger analytics, measurement, and consumer-research methods
→ academic interest
→ marketing analytics / consumer insight / digital marketing analytics
→ career goal
→ consumer insight, market analysis, brand strategy, digital marketing analytics
```

Key questions:
- Why analytics, not only communication or marketing?
- What data, survey, or research foundation exists?
- What gap does the programme fill?
- How will the applicant combine qualitative insight and quantitative analysis?

Note: This track needs evidence such as a research portfolio, survey analysis, SQL/Python/Excel work, or digital marketing measurement. Without it, present it as a stretch track.

Logic chain:

```text
English major
→ language learning, cultural difference, expression training

→ language ability matters in workplace communication, brand expression, cross-cultural coordination
→ reflection
```

Key questions:

### Finance / Accounting / Investment

Logic chain:

```text
Finance/accounting/economics foundation
→ financial statements, valuation, markets, or corporate finance
→ audit/accounting internship
→ financial quality, internal controls, reporting discipline
→ securities/IB/investment/consulting internship
→ transaction logic, valuation, industry research, capital markets
```

Key questions:

- Is the career goal realistic for the region's employment market?

## General Mother Framework

For multi-track applicants, use this as the base framework and adjust the final academic-interest and career-goal segments for each programme. Choose a domain-specific arc rather than forcing every applicant into a humanities or marketing arc.

```text
English major foundation
→ language, culture, and text ability
→ journalism/media experience
→ language in public communication
→ agency/client-facing experience
→ language in brand communication and stakeholder coordination
→ marketing/brand-side experience
→ language, communication, and consumer insight in market decision-making
→ projects or competitions
→ innovation, market research, or analytical evidence
→ graduate study goal
→ systematic training at the intersection of language, communication, and market methods
→ career goal
→ brand communication, international marketing, consumer insight, or professional communication
```

Reusable arc:

```text
Language → Communication → Brand → Consumer Insight
```

Other reusable arcs:

```text
Accounting → Valuation → Investment Judgment → Capital Allocation
Business Problem → Data Method → Decision Insight → Operational Impact
Coursework → Technical Project → Industry Constraint → Engineering Solution
Research Question → Method → Evidence → Future Inquiry
Social Observation → Policy Evidence → Institutional Analysis → Public Impact
Rules → Risk → Governance → Responsible Innovation
```

Programme-specific adjustment:
- Language track: emphasize professional communication, applied language, discourse, and workplace contexts.
- Communication track: emphasize audience, media, narrative, IMC, and strategic communication.
- Marketing track: emphasize consumer insight, brand strategy, market research, and business decision-making.
- Analytics track: emphasize research methods, data, measurement, and evidence-based decision-making.
- Education track: emphasize language learning, curriculum, real-world communication, and learner needs.
- Finance/accounting track: emphasize financial statements, valuation, risk, transactions, and capital allocation.
- Management/strategy track: emphasize business problems, stakeholder coordination, growth, and organizational decisions.
- CS/engineering track: emphasize technical artifacts, systems, constraints, tools, and implementation depth.
- Science/research track: emphasize research questions, methods, evidence, and technical readiness.
- Policy/law track: emphasize institutions, rules, governance, evidence, and public or industry impact.

## Do Not

Do not:
- Copy, paraphrase, or imitate public essays.
- Turn another student's essay into a template.
- Fabricate projects, internships, leadership, awards, or hardship.
- Preserve identifying third-party details.

## Better Prompts

Ask the student:
- What changed after this experience?
- What specific decision or action did you take?
- What evidence proves your capability?
- Why this program, beyond ranking and location?
- What future role does this degree enable?
